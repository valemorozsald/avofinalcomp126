Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Located in Tennessee and North Carolina, this park is known for its misty mountain views, diverse wildlife, and historic buildings.

Everglades National Park

Spanning 1.5 million acres in southern Florida, this park is home to unique wetland ecosystems, including sawgrass marshes, mangrove forests, and cypress swamps.

Cogaree National Park

In South Carolina, Congaree is home to one of the largest remaining old-growth bottomland hardwood forests in the United States, as well as abundant wildlife and hiking trails.

Mammoth Cave National Park

Located in Kentucky, this park is home to the world's longest known cave system, with more than 400 miles of explored passageways. Above ground, visitors can also explore the park's forests and river valleys.
