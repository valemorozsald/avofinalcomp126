Crater Lake National Park

Crater Lake National Park is located in southern Oregon and is known for its stunningly clear blue lake, which is the deepest in the United States. The lake was formed by the collapse of a volcano over 7,000 years ago.

Great Basin National Park

Great Basin National Park is located in eastern Nevada and features a diverse range of natural landscapes, including ancient bristlecone pine forests, underground caves, and the majestic Wheeler Peak. It is a popular destination for hiking, stargazing, and exploring the unique ecology of the Great Basin region.

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park is located on the Big Island of Hawaii and is home to two of the world's most active volcanoes, Kilauea and Mauna Loa. Visitors can witness lava flows, hike through lush rainforests, and explore volcanic craters and steam vents in this unique and ever-changing landscape.

Joshua Tree National Park

Joshua Tree National Park is located in southern California and is named after the iconic Joshua trees that grow throughout the park. The park's unique desert landscape is popular among hikers, rock climbers, and stargazers, with numerous trails and rock formations to explore.
